01 — About

Workshop Overview

Adaptive experimentation — from multi-armed bandits to contextual and causal adaptive designs — is transforming how we collect data and draw conclusions across science, medicine, and technology. Yet fundamental questions remain open: How do we perform valid causal inference under adaptive sampling? What are the efficiency limits? How do we handle delayed or partial feedback?

This workshop brings together researchers from bandit theory, causal inference, semiparametric statistics, and reinforcement learning to take stock of recent progress and chart the path forward. We aim to bridge communities that rarely share a venue, and catalyse collaborations around the shared challenge of learning to act optimally while guaranteeing statistically valid conclusions.

i

Bandit Algorithms

UCB, Thompson Sampling, LinUCB, best-arm identification, regret bounds.

ii

Adaptive Experiments

AIPW, anytime-valid inference, sequential testing, online-to-batch conversion.

iii

Off-Policy Evaluation

Doubly robust estimators, importance weighting, kernel-based OPE.

iv

Causal Inference

Structural causal models, instrumental variables, mediation, causal bandits.

v

Semiparametric Theory

Efficient influence functions, Riesz representers, debiased / double ML.

vi

Applications

Clinical trials, A/B testing at scale, recommender systems, public policy.

04 — Call for Posters

Submit Your Work

We invite poster submissions on recent or ongoing work at the intersection of adaptive experimentation, bandit algorithms, causal inference, and sequential decision-making. Both theoretical and empirical contributions are welcome. Work under review or recently published at a major venue is eligible.

  • Bandit algorithms and regret analysis
  • Causal inference with adaptive data collection
  • Off-policy evaluation and learning
  • Anytime-valid and sequential hypothesis testing
  • Doubly robust and debiased machine learning
  • Reinforcement learning and causal RL
  • Experimental design and sample complexity
  • Real-world applications and case studies
Submission Format Extended abstract · up to 4 pages (excl. references) · PDF · Workshop style. Non-anonymised submissions accepted.
